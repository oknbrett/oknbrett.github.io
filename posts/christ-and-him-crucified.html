1 Corinthians 2:2

Christ and Him Crucified

On the only message that has ever really mattered.

There is one phrase that has been at the center of Christian preaching for two thousand years. Not as a slogan. Not as a warm opener before you get to the real content. As the content.

Paul writes in 1 Corinthians 2:2"For I decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ and him crucified."1 Corinthians 2:2 (ESV): I decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ and him crucified.

That is the whole message. And if that feels too simple, I want to show you why you might be underestimating it.

Why This Phrase Is the Gospel, Not Just a Summary of It

Paul walks into Corinth. A city full of philosophers and intellectuals. People who loved Aristotle, debated stoicism, respected eloquence. They valued wisdom as a status symbol.

And Paul says: yeah, I'm not doing any of that.

He doesn't say it as a one-time thing either. Every city, every crowd, same thing. Galatians 6:14"But far be it from me to boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by which the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world."Galatians 6:14 (ESV). Philippians 3:8"Indeed, I count everything as loss because of the surpassing worth of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord."Philippians 3:8 (ESV). Romans 1:16"For I am not ashamed of the gospel, for it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes."Romans 1:16 (ESV). Christ and Him crucified.

This is not a summary of the gospel before you get to something more advanced. This is the gospel. The whole thing.

"The cross is the centre of the world's history. The incarnation of Christ and the crucifixion of our Lord are the pivot round which all the events of the ages revolve."Alexander Maclaren

Part 1 — Christ: Who He Is

The word Christ is not just a name. It is a title. From the Greek ChristosChristos (Χριστός)The Anointed One. Drawn from the Hebrew Mashiach. The one set apart by God for a specific mission., the Anointed One, the one set apart by God for a specific mission.

Fully God

Almost every religion outside Christianity attacks this. Islam, Jehovah's Witnesses, Mormonism. They will tell you Jesus was great. A prophet. An exalted being. But not God.

Look at Hebrews 1. He is the exact imprint of his nature, upholding the universe by the word of His power. The exact imprint"He is the radiance of the glory of God and the exact imprint of his nature, and he upholds the universe by the word of his power."Hebrews 1:3 (ESV). Not a reflection. Not a resemblance. The exact imprint.

And then God the Father speaks directly to the Son:

"Your throne, O God, is forever and ever."blockedHebrews 1:8 (ESV) Hebrews 1:8 — The Father addressing the Son

The Greek word is TheosTheos (Θεός)The standard Greek word for God throughout the New Testament. The same word applied to God the Father. Not a lesser title.. The Father addresses the Son as God. Paul says in Colossians 2:9"For in him the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily."Colossians 2:9 (ESV) that in Him the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily. The Greek: pleromapleroma (πλήρωμα)Fullness, totality. The complete sum of divine attributes. Nothing of God is held back from Christ.. Everything, in a body.

God did not just send someone. He came. The Creator of the universe came down. He had every right to wipe us out from the moment of Adam and Eve's transgression. He would have been perfectly just. Instead, He came. That is not a small thing.

Fully Man

This part gets less attention than it deserves. He was one of us. Bone of our bone, flesh of our flesh. Real hunger. Real grief. Real sweat. He was deeply distressed in the garden.

"We do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin. Let us therefore with confidence draw near to the throne of grace, that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need."blocked Hebrews 4:15-16 (ESV)

This is not just a comforting detail. It is theologically necessary. Hebrews 2:17"Therefore he had to be made like his brothers in every respect, so that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest."Hebrews 2:17 (ESV) says He had to be made like His brothers in every respect. He had to be one of us to represent us. To stand as us. To succeed where Adam failed.

"He became what we are so that he might make us what he is."Athanasius of Alexandria

Part 2 — Him Crucified: What He Did

Some people like to split God in two. Old Testament: angry and strict. New Testament: gentle and forgiving. The cross destroys that completely.

Both are fully present at Calvary, simultaneously. God's love, mercy, and grace toward sinners. And God's wrath, justice, and hatred of sin toward Christ who bore those sins. Both, fully, at the same time.

The Cross Shows the Full Character of God

Romans 5:8"but God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."Romans 5:8 (ESV): while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. This was not the Son convincing a reluctant Father. The Father, Son, and Spirit agreed on this from before the foundation of the world. Ephesians 1:4"even as he chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him."Ephesians 1:4 (ESV). This was the plan. All of God, loving you.

But God also says in Exodus 34:7"keeping steadfast love for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, but who will by no means clear the guilty..."Exodus 34:7 (ESV): I show steadfast love, forgiving iniquity, but by no means will I clear the guilty. Both. He forgives. And He does not ignore guilt.

Think about it. Imagine you come home and find your entire family murdered. The killer is caught. The judge says: I am a loving God, so I'm letting you go. That is not mercy. That is corruption. Our God is not that judge.

"It was the will of the LORD to crush him; he has put him to grief."blocked Isaiah 53:10 (ESV)

This was not an accident. God directed His wrath, purposefully, justly, onto His own Son. Romans 3:25"whom God put forward as a propitiation by his blood, to be received by faith."Romans 3:25 (ESV) says God put Christ forward as a hilasterionhilasterion (ἱλαστήριον)The mercy seat. The place where wrath was satisfied. Paul says Christ is that place., the mercy seat. God did not cancel His wrath. He directed it onto His own Son.

"On the cross, God loved us enough to give his Son, and he was holy enough to punish sin in his Son."John Stott

Substitutionary Atonement: He Stood in Your Place

1 Timothy 2:6"who gave himself as a ransom for all, which is the testimony given at the proper time."1 Timothy 2:6 (ESV): Christ gave himself as a ransom, antilytronantilytron (ἀντίλυτρον)A ransom in place of another. The prefix anti means "instead of." Not alongside, not merely with — but as a substitute., a ransom in place of. Not alongside you. Not with you. Instead of you.

For every sin you have committed, and every sin you will commit, there is a punishment owed. Christ took every single one. Not a general death. The exact punishment that was yours, He took personally. He did not drink a general cup of wrath. He drank yours.

"He was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities."blocked Isaiah 53:5 (ESV)

2 Corinthians 5:21"For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God."2 Corinthians 5:21 (ESV): God made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us. He was not sinful. He was and is and will always be sinless. But on the cross, He was legally declared as bearing every charge, every punishment, so that you could be legally declared righteous. Your sin moved to Him. His righteousness moved to you. That exchange is the gospel.

The forgiveness was accomplished on the cross. Not offered pending your response. Accomplished. When someone says it is not fair for a bad person to go to heaven: those sins were punished. On Christ. It is just that the punishment was not yours. That is not unfairness. That is mercy.

The Theological Point: This Is Enough

Paul is not saying this as an opener. He is saying this is the content. Complete. Sufficient. Nothing to add. 1 Corinthians 1:30"And because of him you are in Christ Jesus, who became to us wisdom from God, righteousness and sanctification and redemption."1 Corinthians 1:30 (ESV): Christ became for us wisdom from God, and righteousness and sanctification and redemption. All of it. In Him. The cross is not the beginning of something better. The cross is the good stuff.

"I have a great need for Christ; I have a great Christ for my need."Charles Spurgeon

What Do You Do With This?

The work is done. He stood in your place. That is settled. So what does that call you to? Not obligation. Not transaction. It calls you to something simpler and harder: gratitude that changes how you live.

You cannot repay this. Give eternity times eternity and you still cannot match it. Stop trying to balance the ledger. Live from gratitude. Let that truth reshape how you spend your time, how you treat people, how you pray, what you pursue.

And in your suffering, come to Him. He is not distant from your pain. He knows what it feels like.

"Let us therefore with confidence draw near to the throne of grace, that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need."blocked Hebrews 4:16 (ESV)

And rest in this: if you are truly in Christ, nothing can separate you from His love. Romans 8:38-39"For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."Romans 8:38-39 (ESV). He loved you enough to die for you. He rose to secure you.

The message is still the same as it was when Paul walked into Corinth.

Christ and Him crucified.

It was enough then. It is enough now.

May the peace and love of God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ be with you.
